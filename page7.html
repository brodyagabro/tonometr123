HOW TO MEASURE THE PRESSURE CORRECTLY WITH A MECHANICAL TONOMETER?

Many people who suffer from heart and vascular diseases have to monitor blood pressure (BP). If you do not do this, you can skip the occurrence of complications, such as hypertension or hypotension. In this situation, a device for measuring blood pressure – a mechanical tonometer can help. It shows fairly accurate results and is inexpensive, but it is more difficult to use such a device than semi-automatic and automatic. To solve this problem, you can search for information on the Internet or visit a doctor who will tell you how to properly measure blood pressure with a mechanical type of tonometer.

The principle of operation of the device The device for measuring blood pressure, regardless of whether it is mechanical or automatic, works according to one principle, namely: A cuff is put on. Air is pumped into it to compress the artery. The air is gradually removed from the cuff and the first pulse beat is recorded, which is the upper blood pressure (systolic). The last stroke shows the lower (diastolic) blood pressure. The value between the upper and lower indicator is the pulse pressure. All modern semi-automatic, automatic and mechanical tonometers work according to this principle. Sometimes the tonometer shows different pressure on the right and left hand. If the difference does not exceed 15 units, then there is usually nothing to worry about, but when it is greater than this indicator, then you need to be examined. Differences in pressure can be caused by malfunctions in the work of internal organs, VSD, diseases of the cardiovascular system, as well as banal stress and fatigue. A slight increase in the indicators on the left hand, especially if a person is left-handed, is allowed due to the nutrition of this limb by the aorta itself. To understand how to properly measure the pressure yourself, you need to familiarize yourself in more detail with the features of the selected device. It is equally important to study the rules that must be followed to obtain accurate data.
