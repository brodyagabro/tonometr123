What is a blood pressure monitor? Types of tonometers

A tonometer is a medical device designed to measure the arterial (blood) pressure of a patient at home and in medical institutions. The tonometer consists of a cuff worn on the patient's shoulder or wrist, a device (manual or automatic) for pumping air into the cuff, a pressure gauge or an electronic sensor measuring the air pressure in the cuff. Also, the tonometer is equipped with either a stethoscope or an electronic device that registers air pulsations in the cuff. In the latter case, the measurement result is displayed on the screen of the electronic unit.

Tonometers are divided into the following main types:

- Mechanical (CS Medica CS 105, CS Medica CS 109 Pro, B.Well MED-62, Microlife BP AG1-30, A&D UA-100 or Little Doctor LD-71). The principle of operation is based on the auscultative method of measuring blood pressure, in which the moments of the appearance and disappearance of noises (Korotkov tones) at the opening of the brachial artery, manifested by a decrease in excess pressure in the inflated cuff, are recorded using a stethoscope and correlated with the readings of the pressure gauge. The injection of air into the cuff worn on the patient's shoulder is carried out manually, using a special pear. Advantages: a high degree of confidence in the results obtained and the low price of the device. Disadvantages: if the measurement is not carried out by a qualified specialist, there is a high probability of making one or more mistakes, in particular, incorrectly determining Korotkov tones, allowing excessive compression of air into the cuff, decompressing air from the cuff too quickly or too slowly. The final measurement error is 5-15 mm Hg.

- Automatic. The principle of operation is based on an oscillometric method of measuring blood pressure, in which fluctuations (oscillations) of blood pressure in the brachial artery cause air fluctuations in the cuff, which is fixed on the patient's arm. Fluctuations are recorded by the pressure sensor of the device, processed by a microprocessor, and the result of calculations is displayed on the screen of the electronic unit of the tonometer. The injection of air into the cuff worn on the patient's shoulder or wrist is performed automatically. Advantages: reducing the influence of the human factor on the process of registering blood pressure, resistance to noise, the ability to measure even through thin clothing, the absence of failures and errors in the presence of pronounced "auscultative failure" and weak Korotkov tones, simple use without the need for special skills. Disadvantages: immobility during measurement, obtaining inaccurate results in patients with cardiovascular problems. The final measurement error is 2-5 mm Hg.

Automatic tonometers are divided into two main types: with cuff fixation on the shoulder (Omron M2 Basic, Omron M2 Classic, A&D UA-888E, A&D UA-777, Little Doctor LD3A or B.Well Pro 33) and on the wrist (Omron RS2, A&D UB-202, Nissei WS-820 or B.Well MED-57).

- Semi-automatic (Omron S1, A&D UA-705, Little Doctor LD2 or B.Well PRO-30). The principle of operation is also based on the oscillometric method of measuring blood pressure, but the injection of air into the cuff worn on the patient's shoulder is performed manually using a special pear. The disadvantage compared to automatic tonometers is the need for self-injection of air into the cuff, which can lead to an overestimation of the indicators by 10-15 mm Hg. Advantage: low price.

Semi-automatic and automatic tonometers form a single group called electronic tonometers.
