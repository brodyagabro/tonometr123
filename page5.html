How to choose a blood pressure monitor for home use

A tonometer is a special medical device designed to measure blood pressure. Blood pressure is an important indicator of the state of the cardiovascular system.

The presence of a blood pressure monitor in your home is a prerequisite for preventing the risks of possible health problems. Before you buy it, you need to familiarize yourself with the list of necessary criteria that a modern device must meet.

How to choose a blood pressure monitor for home use

Mechanical tonometer
The device of this type has long confirmed the reputation of a reliable and unpretentious tonometer for home use. It costs, as a rule, inexpensive.

Components of the device:

cuff;
pear;
a stethoscope.
Note. It is not difficult to acquire skills for measuring (if you have good hearing and vision), but a mechanical tonometer is not quite convenient for individual use. It is necessary to adapt to this process and take into account possible errors in the readings due to the physical effort expended.

Popular manufacturers of mechanical tonometers:

B. Well (models WM-62 S, WM-61)
Microlife (models BP AG1-20, BP AG1-40, BP AG1-30)
CS Medica (models CS-105, CS-106, CS-107)
Electronic tonometer
The devices are divided into three types – semi-automatic, automatic and wrist devices.

The correct choice of an electronic tonometer depends on a number of factors:

patient's age;
specific features of his physique;
health conditions, including vision and other factors;
forms of the disease.
For example, semi-automatic machines have the same drawback as conventional tonometers – inaccuracy of results in individual measurement of blood pressure. In automatic machines, the air in the cuff is inflated by a compressor, but they are not suitable for elderly people. Doctors also do not recommend wrist devices for them, as their readings are not always accurate. But these devices are popular with athletes and young people.

Popular manufacturers of electronic tonometers:

AMD (models UA-888, UA-777, UB-202)
Omron (models M2 Basic (7121), M2 Classic)
B. Well (models PRO-33, WA-55, PRO-35)
Conclusions
Choosing the most convenient tonometer /catalog / tonometry/ for you, proceed from all of the above factors. There are no unambiguous and universal recommendations. Take into account your individual characteristics! Consult your doctor and check all types of tonometers in practice.
