Features of a mechanical device for determining blood pressure

According to one principle of operation, it is difficult to understand how to choose a tonometer for measuring pressure, so experts advise to study the features of each model. The mechanical appearance of the device is a set of tubes connecting a pear that pumps air and drains it, with a cuff squeezing the arteries. Even the best models of this type are cheap enough, but to measure the pressure, you will have to additionally take a stethoscope. It is applied to the artery to hear the first and last beat. A mechanical tonometer is often sold together with a stethoscope, so it is advisable to learn about this nuance before buying. Measuring blood pressure requires accuracy and professional skills, so not every type of device is suitable for a beginner. Mechanical tonometers can show the most truthful results and, when used correctly, actually have no errors. However, if you give such a measuring device to an inexperienced person, or a person with poor eyesight and hearing, then the indicators may not be completely accurate. That is why, before buying a mechanical device, it is necessary to take into account the possible human factor, since it is not easy to measure pressure with it. There are simplified versions of a mechanical tonometer, for example, with a combined pear and a pressure gauge or a stethoscope head sewn into the cuff. Measuring pressure with such modifications is much easier and faster. In general, measuring pressure with a mechanical tonometer has such advantages: It is always an accurate result if you figure out how to measure pressure correctly with a mechanical tonometer. In the presence of diseases of the cardiovascular system and arrhythmia, the device shows truthful indicators and without errors. Low price, compared to other types of measuring instruments. In some situations, it is extremely inconvenient to measure pressure with a mechanical tonometer, namely: poor eyesight; hearing loss; problems with fine motor skills. If there is a desire to buy medical tonometers for measuring pressure, then the mechanical option is one of them. Most modern hospitals work with such devices. After all, the experience of doctors allows them to get the most accurate information without using an electronic tonometer. Tonometers are selected for measuring pressure not only by features, but also by size. A standard cuff does not exceed 30 cm, a large one – 45 cm, and a children's one - no more than 22 cm. If the size does not fit, then the measurement of blood pressure will be inaccurate.
